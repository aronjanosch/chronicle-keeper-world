---
name: Flesh out a place
description: Worldbuilding questions for a location — geography, history, culture, daily life, conflict. Use when the user wants to develop or deepen a place, region, or settlement page.
kinds: [place, region, settlement]
---

Help the user deepen a place page. Read its body first (read_page) so you build on
what is already there — never re-ask what the page answers.

How to work:
- Pick the 2–4 questions below that the page is most silent on and that matter most for
  play, and raise those. Do not dump the whole list — that is drudgery.
- These are prompts for the user, not a form to fill. Ask, let them answer in their own
  words, then offer to write it into the page (you draft, they approve). Always skippable.
- Favour detail that creates story: tension, hooks, things a party would notice or trip
  over. Skip trivia that never comes up at the table.
- When you do write, fit the established world and the page's kind (check page_kinds for
  its infobox fields).

Questions to draw from:

**Place in the world**
- Where is it, and what is it near or far from that matters (trade, threats, rivals)?
- Why is it here? What does the land give it — water, ore, a pass, a harbour, a holy site?
- Who rules or owns it, and who really holds power day to day?

**Look and feel**
- What does a traveller see, hear, and smell on first arriving?
- What is the architecture, layout, and state of repair? Rich, poor, fortified, sprawling?
- What is the climate and season's effect on daily life?

**People and culture**
- Who lives here, and how do they make a living?
- What do locals value, fear, or gossip about? Customs a stranger would get wrong?
- Factions, classes, or groups in tension within it?

**History**
- What founded it, and what is the worst thing that ever happened here?
- What ruins, scars, monuments, or grudges does the past leave visible?

**Conflict and hooks**
- What problem is unsolved right now? Who wants what, and who stands in the way?
- What would bring adventurers here — and what danger is under the surface?
- A secret the place keeps? (Consider a `[!secret]` callout for GM eyes.)
